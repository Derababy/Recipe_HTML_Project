SPAGHETTI
BOLOGNESE		 The history of the bolognese sauce started in the late 18th century. The first mention of the term “alla Bolognese” appeared in Pellegrino Artusi's cookbook. Historians generally agree that the dish originated in Imola, a city that sits just west of Bologna, and is home to the earliest documented ragù sauce. Bolognese sauce is an italian dish made up of meat-based tomato sauce spiced with herbs and served with spaghetti. Hence, Spaghetti Bolognese is basically serving spaghetti with the Bolognese sauce. The bolognese sauce has evolved over the years, with every cook putting their own spin on it.

INGREDIENTS

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 500g minced beef
  • 2 medium onions, diced
  • 2 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery sticks, chopped
  • 2 x 400g tin plum tomato
  • 2 tbsp tomato puree
  • 1 tsp dried oregano and mixed herbs
  • 2 red chili pepper, chopped
  • 300ml stock or 125ml red wine
  • Stock cube (ideally beef, but any will do)
  • 400g spaghetti
  • Salt to taste
  • 75g parmesan cheese, grated (optional)

DIRECTIONS

  1. Heat the olive oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the onions and saute until lightly golden. Add the garlic and fry for 4-5 minutes.
  2. Add the minced beef and fry until fully cooked and brown.
  3. Then add the chili pepper, oregano and mixed herbs and cook for another 10 minutes.
  4. Stir in the tomatoes, beef stock/wine and tomato puree. Season with salt and stock cube.
  5. Bring to boil, then reduce the heat and simmer, add the carrots and celery sticks stirring occasionally for 15 minutes.
  6. Meanwhile, in a large pan, add 500ml of water and bring to boil. Put the spaghetti in the boiling water and add a teaspoon of salt.
  7. Cook till tender. Drain well and run hot water through it. Put it back in the pan and add a dash of olive oil and stir thoroughly.
  8. Serve the spaghetti and bolognese sauce. Sprinkle Parmesan cheese on top if you desire. Enjoy!
